About Our Academy

Nritya Kala is dedicated to preserving India's sacred dance heritage. For over 15 years, we have trained students in classical and folk traditions—from the royal courts of Rajasthan to the temple stages of Tamil Nadu and Kerala.

We follow the guru-shishya parampara (teacher-student tradition), teaching mudras, expressions, rhythm, and costume with respect for each region's culture. Beginners, children, and advanced learners are all welcome.

  • Classical & folk forms under one roof
  • Annual recitals during Navratri & cultural festivals
  • Morning and evening batches available

Dance Forms We Teach

Five beautiful traditions from across India—each with its own story, rhythm, and grace.

Ghoomar

A graceful folk dance from Rajasthan, performed in swirling ghagras during festivals and weddings.

Kathak

North India's storytelling dance, known for chakkars (spins), tabla rhythms, and expressive abhinaya.

Garba

Gujarat's joyful circle dance for Navratri, danced with dandiya sticks to honour Goddess Durga.

Kathakali

Kerala's dramatic dance-drama with elaborate makeup, mudras, and tales from the Ramayana and Mahabharata.

Bharatanatyam

Tamil Nadu's ancient temple dance, celebrated for precise footwork, aramandi, and sacred mudras.
